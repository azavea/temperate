Frequently asked questions

  • Who should use Temperate?

    Temperate was designed to help people with limited resources doing climate change adaptation planning at the city or local level. There’s no need to have any scientific expertise or other specific background knowledge. Simply create an account and we’ll walk you through it from there!

  • What if data for my city is not in Temperate?

    Temperate currently features more than 1,000 cities in the United States. If you’re interested in using Temperate but we don’t have data on your city, let us know! We are actively seeking funding and this information will help us with our efforts.

  • My city has signed the Global Covenant of Mayors for Climate and Energy. Can I still use Temperate?

    Yes! Temperate uses the same hazards and community systems that the Global Covenant asks their signatories to assess.

  • Where does your data come from?

    See our Methodology page.

  • Where do your “suggested actions” come from?

    The suggested actions in Temperate come from over 40 cities across the United States, as compiled by Dr. Melissa Stults. See the Methodology page for more in-depth information. As more people use Temperate, we hope to see them add actions that are “public” to other Temperate users. Each action will be evaluated by the experts at ICLEI-USA before being included in Temperate as a suggested action.

  • What happens to my work if I don’t upgrade at the end of my free trial?

    You can export the information you entered as a CSV file. Any actions you created and tagged as public may still be available to users of Temperate.

    We’d love to hear it! Please contact us here.
